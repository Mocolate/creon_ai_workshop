Use whatever's easiest for the thing in front of you.

## Warm-up — Extract your brand voice as rules *(~10 min)*

The output of this warm-up becomes the foundation of whichever path you pick — your brand voice as a saved document Claude can use everywhere.

#### Step 0 — Gather 3 to 5 pieces of marketing copy (max 5 min)
You want a small, varied set of writing that genuinely *sounds like Creon*. Think: your own drive, Instagram (@creonkozijnen), the Creon Online website, recent newsletters.

Grab as many as you want — the more the better — but 3 to 5 should be enough. Variety helps. Set a 5-minute timer.

#### Step 1. Open a new chat in Claude.

#### Step 2. Paste your marketing copy.
Then say:

> *"These are pieces of marketing copy I've written for Creon that I'm happy with.*
>
> *Read them and describe my brand voice as a clear set of rules. Be specific. I'm looking for things like:*
> *- Tone and energy.*
> *- Typical sentence length and structure.*
> *- Words I tend to use vs words I avoid.*
> *- How I handle CTAs (call-to-action).*
> *- What I do consistently across all of them.*
>
> *Output it as a clean reference document I can save."*

#### Step 3. Push back on anything that's off.
> *"You said I use exclamation marks often — actually I almost never use them. Re-examine the examples and correct."*

#### Step 4. Ask Claude to fill its own gaps.
> *"Ask me questions about any gaps or uncertainties in this brand voice description — anything you had to guess at. Keep asking until you have a clear picture."*

Answer the questions. This catches the things your examples didn't show.

#### Step 5. Save the final version.
Copy the text and save it as a file (or ask Claude to give it to you as an Artifact you can download). Call it something like **`creon_brand_voice.md`**.

**What you just did.** Instead of describing your voice from scratch — and probably leaving things out — you let Claude read your work and surface the rules from the examples. That's the "extract then apply" pattern. The document it produced is grounded in your actual writing.

**You'll upload this `creon_brand_voice.md` into the Project in whichever path you pick.**

### Path 1 — Build your next Instagram posts *(~25 min)*

**You'll leave with:** a Marketing Project loaded with your voice and your recent posts, that drafts new posts you'd actually want to publish.

#### Step 0 — Gather your materials (max 5 min)
**Minimum to start with:**
- The **`creon_brand_voice.md`** you produced in the warm-up — most important.
- 3-5 of your recent Instagram captions.

**Ideal additions** (only if quick to find):
- Brand guidelines or visual identity doc.
- A short Creon products / positioning / typical customer note. Even rough bullet points are fine — you can also type this directly into the Project instructions instead.

Don't go past 5 minutes on gathering. You can always add more files to a Project later.

#### Step 1. Create the Project.
- Click **Projects** in the sidebar, then **New project**.
- Name it: **Amber — Creon Social**.

#### Step 2. Upload your materials.
Upload what you gathered in Step 0. Start with the minimum.

#### Step 3. Give the Project its instructions.
Paste this into the **Custom instructions** and adjust:

> *"You help me draft Instagram content for Creon. You hold my brand voice rules, my recent posts and our product positioning in this Project.*
>
> *When I ask for a post, always offer 3 caption variations: short / medium / long.*
>
> *Always include:*
> *- A clear hook in the first line.*
> *- A one-line content brief for the visual to go with the caption.*
> *- 3 to 5 hashtag suggestions, all on-brand.*
>
> *Stay human. No AI-sounding phrasing. No corporate jargon. Match the voice rules in the uploaded document.*
>
> *I'll always edit before posting. Treat your output as a first draft, not a final version.*
>
> *If you're not sure what direction I'm going for (campaign theme, product, season), ask me before writing."*

#### Step 4. Test it.
Start a new chat inside the Project. Try a real upcoming need:

> *"I need a post announcing our new colour range for ALU windows. Soft launch — we want to build curiosity without saying everything yet."*

The prompt can also be fully open. Upload an image you're thinking of posting and ask Claude to brainstorm with you — what's the angle, what could the caption be, what hook would work, what audience would respond. Use it as a thinking partner, not just a caption generator.

#### Step 5. Refine.
Push back on anything that smells AI:

> *"The first line sounds generic. Look at my last 5 posts — none of them start like that. Try again."*
>
> *"Drop the word 'innovative' — I never use it."*

Update the Project instructions with whatever rules emerge from the refinement.

#### Stretch goal
Add an instruction: *"When I ask for a campaign across multiple platforms, also produce the adapted versions for LinkedIn (longer, more professional) and TikTok (shorter, hookier, less polished)."*

### Path 2 — Co-design a 90-day social media strategy *(~30 min)*

**You'll leave with:** a tailored 90-day social media strategy document for Creon — built by working *with* Claude as a thinking partner, not by asking it to generate a strategy.

This is the path where pushback matters most. Claude's first draft will be generic. Half of this path is arguing with it.

#### Step 1. Open a new chat in Claude.

#### Step 2. Brief Claude on Creon.
Paste what you know:
- Products: PVC and aluminium window frames, doors, sliding doors, rolling shutters, window decor, façade cladding.
- Audience: who buys window frames? (Be specific — homeowners renovating, installation businesses, consumer vs. business buyers?)
- Current channels and roughly how each is performing.
- Goals for the next 90 days — even rough ones.

Upload your **`creon_brand_voice.md`** from the warm-up.

#### Step 3. Ask for the interview first.
> *"Before drafting any strategy, interview me. Ask me up to 10 questions covering anything you need to know to write a strategy that's specific to Creon, not generic to 'a window company'. Don't write the strategy yet."*

#### Step 4. Answer the questions honestly.
Short answers are fine. The point is to surface what you actually know vs what's a guess.

#### Step 5. Ask for the strategy.
> *"Now produce a 90-day social media strategy as a structured document. Cover: monthly themes, posting cadence per channel, content pillars, influencer angles, success metrics. Make it specific to Creon — not a template a competitor could use."*

#### Step 6. Argue with it.
This is the critical step. Read what Claude wrote and **push back on every generic line**:

> *"'Engage your community' isn't a strategy. What specifically should I be doing on Tuesday mornings?"*
>
> *"This pillar — 'product education' — looks like every other manufacturer. Where's the angle that's actually us?"*
>
> *"I don't have time for this much volume. Cut it in half and tell me which posts matter most."*

Each round of pushback makes it more specific to Creon.

#### Step 7. Save it.
Ask Claude to output the final version as a clean document Artifact you can download.

#### Stretch goal
Once the strategy exists, ask: *"What would week 1 actually look like? Give me the exact 4 posts I'd publish, when, on which platform."*

### Path 3 — Build your customer call prep co-pilot *(~25 min)*

**You'll leave with:** a workflow that condenses pre-call thinking into focused prep, fast.

Your outbound advisory calls — where you help new customers and gently convince them — are sensitive work. Structured prep is something Claude handles well.

*A note about materials:* most of what makes you good at these calls lives in your head, not in documents. So this path uses a different pattern from Paths 1 and 2 — instead of uploading examples, you'll *describe* a recent call in conversation with Claude, and let Claude turn that into reusable context for the Project.

#### Step 0 — Pick one recent call to think about (max 3 min)
Choose a call from the last few weeks that was either:
- A typical advisory call that went well, OR
- A call that didn't go the way you wanted.

You'll describe it to Claude. No need to write anything down first.

#### Step 1. Create the Project.
- Name it: **Amber — Customer Calls**.

#### Step 2. Upload the minimum.
Just the **`creon_brand_voice.md`** for now. We'll let the Project learn the rest through conversation.

#### Step 3. Tell Claude about the call.
Start a new chat inside the Project. Say:

> *"I want to build a co-pilot for prepping my outbound advisory calls to Creon customers. Let me tell you about a recent call so you understand what these calls are like.*
>
> *[Describe: who you called, what they had bought or were considering, how you opened, what they were worried about, what you said, how it ended.]*
>
> *Now ask me 5–7 follow-up questions to make sure you understand my typical call shape, my tone, and what makes a call go well vs. badly."*

Answer the questions. Be specific.

#### Step 4. Ask Claude to write the Project's reference notes.
> *"Based on what I just told you, write a one-page reference document that captures: what kind of calls these are, who Creon's typical customers are, what my tone is, what 'good' looks like for me. I'll save this into the Project for future runs."*

Save the document — copy and paste it as a file into the Project, or ask Claude to give it to you as an Artifact you can download and re-upload.

#### Step 5. Give the Project its standing instructions.
> *"You help me prep for outbound customer calls. You hold our products, my typical customer types, and notes on how I run these calls.*
>
> *When I give you a customer to prep for, suggest:*
> *1. A warm, natural opening line — not scripted.*
> *2. 3 questions I should ask to understand their needs.*
> *3. Likely objections they might have and how I'd respond.*
> *4. A soft CTA at the end of the call.*
>
> *Keep it conversational, never salesy. Always remind me that the goal is to help, not to push."*

#### Step 6. Test it with an upcoming call.
> *"I'm about to call [Customer A]. Here's what I know: they bought [product] last month, no contact since, no complaints flagged. The call is to check satisfaction and mention our extended warranty programme."*

#### Step 7. Refine.
Adjust until the prep matches how you actually talk:

> *"'How are you feeling about your new windows' is too generic. Replace with something more specific to what they bought."*

#### Stretch goal — add the debrief
Add a post-call instruction to the Project:
> *"When I paste my notes after a call, give me back: a structured summary of what was discussed, any commitments I made, and the 1-2 follow-up actions I need to take."*

Then you have one Project that helps before *and* after the call.

### Path 4 — Visual mockups for developers, with care *(extension reading, only if you're confident)*

**You already do this.** This is here for completeness, not as a recommended path for today.

You currently use Claude to make quick HTML mockups for developers. That's already good — but there's a real risk to be aware of: as Artifacts get bigger, they get unfamiliar. You ask for one change, it touches something else, and suddenly you're scrolling through code you can't read trying to find what broke. **Even experienced engineers get this feeling.**

**If you want to push this path:**

- **Keep mockups small and well-bounded.** One component at a time, not a full page in one go.
- **Always ask Claude to explain each change in plain language before applying it.** *"Before changing anything, tell me in two sentences what you'll change and what won't change."*
- **Save versions.** Before any significant iteration, ask Claude to give you the current version as a download. If the next change breaks something, you can go back.
- **Don't trust the visual alone.** Ask Claude: *"Did you change anything other than what I asked for?"* and read the answer carefully.

This path is better explored at your own pace, after the workshop, when you can take time to build the rhythm.
